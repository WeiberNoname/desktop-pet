================================================================================
PLAN T009: REAL-WORLD PATTERN REPLICATION & BLENDER-STYLE 3D NAVIGATION
================================================================================

This document details:
1. 5 creative concepts to enhance user engagement through realistic pattern replication,
   including a FIFA-style collectible chemistry mechanics concept.
2. 3 distinct technical alternatives to implement mouse-driven Blender-style 
   navigation (orbit, pan, and zoom) for custom mascot view perspectives.

================================================================================
PART 1: 5 CREATIVE CONCEPTS FOR ENGAGEMENT & PATTERN REPLICATION
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. CONCEPT 1: ULTIMATE COMPANION CARDS & CHEMISTRY (FIFA-RELATED ALTERNATIVE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This concept replicates the collectors' loop, ratings system, and synergy matching
of professional football team builders (like FIFA Ultimate Team) into the desktop
pet ecosystem.

* How it works:
  - Collectible Cards: The settings panel includes a "Card Deck" view. Every loaded 
    mascot (standard or custom GLB) is assigned a collectible "Player Card" design 
    (e.g., Bronze, Silver, Gold, or special "Team of the Week" cards) with visible 
    ratings and statistics (e.g., Energy, Speed, Love, Intellect).
  - Mascot Packs: Users earn in-game points (by keeping the app active, completing 
    wellness goals, or clicking the pet) to open virtual "Mascot Packs." These pack 
    animations reveal new procedural skins, accessory models (hats, soccer shirts, 
    glasses), or special animation actions.
  - Squad Chemistry: If the user runs multiple pets (if multiple windows are spawned) 
    or dresses them in matching accessories (e.g., same club colors), they receive 
    "Chemistry Links." Higher chemistry unlocks unique cooperative animations, such 
    as pets passing a 3D soccer ball back and forth, or performing a synchronized celebration.

* Real-World Pattern Replicated:
  - The human desire for collecting, trading, cataloging (the Panini / Trading Card pattern), 
    and systemic team-synergy optimization (chemistry).

* Pros & Cons:
  - Pros: High replay value, extremely addictive progression loop, and viral sharing potential.
  - Cons: Requires UI screens inside Electron for card displays and pack opening.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. CONCEPT 2: ENVIRONMENTAL & WEATHER SYNCHRONICITY (BIOLOGICAL & PHYSICAL SYNC)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Replicate the physical changes of the local outdoor climate directly on the user's 
desktop, binding the pet's behaviors to the real-world weather patterns.

* How it works:
  - Weather Detection: Query a free geolocation or weather API using Electron's 
    main process on startup, matching the pet's environment to the user's city.
  - Weather Adaptation:
    - Rainy/Stormy: The pet puts on a tiny 3D yellow raincoat/umbrella. Raindrops 
      splash off its collision capsule, and it enters a "cozy/lazy" sleepy state.
    - Snowy: The pet shivers, breathes tiny visible steam particles, and can be clicked 
      to throw a snowball at your cursor.
    - Sunny/Hot: The pet wears sunglasses, pants, and sits next to a tiny fan.
  - Circadian Rhythm: Light levels in the pet's WebGL viewport dim at dusk, and tiny 
    fireflies float around it at night.

* Real-World Pattern Replicated:
  - The biological adaptability of organisms to their local climate and circadian cycles, 
    fostering a shared physical connection between user and mascot.

* Pros & Cons:
  - Pros: Deepens the sense of the pet "co-existing" in the user's actual room.
  - Cons: Requires an internet connection and external API keys for weather queries.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. CONCEPT 3: DESKTOP GRAVITY & ACCIDENTAL PHYSICS (PHYSICAL KINEMATICS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Transform the mascot from a floating visual overlay into an active participant in 
the desktop's physical coordinate space, subject to gravity and collision bounds.

* How it works:
  - Gravity Engine: Instead of a static window, the pet's container is pulled downwards 
    by a virtual gravity force, resting on top of the taskbar or active program windows.
  - Flinging & Drag: Dragging the pet with the cursor builds velocity. Releasing the mouse 
    "throws" the pet across the screen. It bounces off screen boundaries (left, right, top) 
    and lands with a squash-and-stretch impact animation.
  - Interactive Panic: While airborne or falling, the pet plays a comedic, flailing 
    animation, and dust particles puff upon hitting the desktop ground.
  - Taskbar Patrol: The pet walks along the top edge of the Windows taskbar, occasionally 
    tripping or sitting down on the corner of active apps.

* Real-World Pattern Replicated:
  - Classical Newtonian mechanics (gravity, velocity, momentum, elastic collisions).

* Pros & Cons:
  - Pros: Creates a highly interactive, toy-like tactile feedback loop that users can play 
    with while waiting for compiles or downloads.
  - Cons: Real-time window positioning at high framerates can lead to window jitter on slower systems.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. CONCEPT 4: MIRRORED PRODUCTIVITY & WORK COMPANIONSHIP (BEHAVIORAL REFLECTION)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Replicate human labor and concentration patterns, making the pet mirror the user's 
current focus levels and work style.

* How it works:
  - Mirror Mode: Electron tracks keyboard and mouse activity levels. If the user is 
    typing furiously (e.g. coding or writing), the pet spawns a tiny 3D desk/computer 
    and typing animations, typing in sync with the user.
  - Tiredness & Coffee: If typing continues uninterrupted for over an hour, the pet's 
    speed slows down, and it starts yawning or slumping. Clicking it spawns a cup of coffee, 
    re-energizing it.
  - Focus Pomodoro: The pet runs a visible Pomodoro timer. During focus times, it works 
    silently. During break times, it rings a bell, stands up, stretches, walks around the 
    screen, and pops up speech bubbles suggesting the user drink water or do hand stretches.

* Real-World Pattern Replicated:
  - The psychological concept of mirroring (mimicry) in human companionship, and the 
    natural cycle of cognitive focus and fatigue.

* Pros & Cons:
  - Pros: Actually helps the user's health and productivity; feels like a true partner in work.
  - Cons: Requires setting up lightweight hook listeners for mouse/keyboard inputs without triggering anti-virus warnings.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. CONCEPT 5: FREQUENCY DANCER & AUDIO RESONANCE (ACOUSTIC VIBRATION)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Make the pet's movement, energy, and visual effects react dynamically to the audio 
frequencies and music vibrating through the user's speakers.

* How it works:
  - Audio Capture: Access system audio output via Electron's WebAudio APIs (with loopback 
    or microphone permission).
  - Beat Reactivity: The pet's bobbing speed, vertical jumping, scale pulsation, and 
    emitted particle effects sync directly to the bass and high notes of the playing audio.
  - Music Genres:
    - High-Tempo (EDM/Rock): The pet dances, spins, or plays a tiny air guitar.
    - Low-Tempo (Lo-Fi/Classical): The pet sways gently, floats, or falls asleep.
    - Silent: The pet goes back to its standard idle breathing cycles.

* Real-World Pattern Replicated:
  - The physical resonance of waves (cymatics) and the universal human response of moving 
    to rhythm and music.

* Pros & Cons:
  - Pros: Turns the mascot into a gorgeous, dynamic ambient audio visualizer.
  - Cons: Capturing system audio can require extra permissions or specialized setup on Windows.

PART 2: BLENDER-STYLE 3D NAVIGATION CONTROLS (ORBIT, PAN, ZOOM)
================================================================================
To give users full control over the 3D model's orientation, scale, and focus point 
directly inside the transparent canvas, we propose 3 implementation methods.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ALTERNATIVE A: THREE.JS ORBITCONTROLS INTEGRATION (CAMERA-CENTRIC TARGET SYSTEM)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This solution implements the official `OrbitControls` addon inside `renderer.js`. It
shifts the Three.js camera around a defined focal target based on mouse coordinates.

* Mouse Interaction Mapping (Blender Sync):
  - Orbit (Rotate View): Middle Mouse Button (MMB) + Drag.
  - Pan (Up/Down/Left/Right/Forward/Backward on camera grid): Shift + MMB + Drag.
  - Zoom (Move Forward/Backward): Scroll Wheel.
  
* Electron Integration Flow:
  1. Detect keydown / mouse events inside the transparent window.
  2. If the user presses the `Alt` or `Ctrl` key, or interacts with mouse clicks:
     - IPC fires: `ipcRenderer.send('set-ignore-mouse', false)` to focus mouse on Electron.
     - Temporarily disable the drag-to-move window logic.
  3. Feed raw mouse events directly into the `OrbitControls` listener.
  4. On keyup or mouseleave, re-enable standard click-through and window movement.

* Pros:
  - Uses standard, highly optimized, and tested camera math out of the box.
  - Supports automatic easing (damping/inertia) for smooth rotation and movement.
* Cons:
  - Camera translation can easily move the mascot outside the bounds of the Electron window
    (clipping borders) if not hard-constrained.
  - Requires loading an additional JS module (`three/addons/controls/OrbitControls.js`).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ALTERNATIVE B: CUSTOM RAW MATRIX TRANSFORMATIONS (MODEL-CENTRIC SYSTEM)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Instead of moving the camera, this approach keeps the camera locked and directly
manipulates the translation matrix of the model's outer group (`characterGroup`).

* Mouse Interaction Mapping (Blender Sync):
  - Orbit (Rotate View): Left Click + Mouse Drag while holding `Alt` (adjusts model rotation).
  - Pan (Up/Down/Forward/Backward): Left Click + Mouse Drag while holding `Shift` (adjusts model position).
  - Zoom (Move Forward/Backward): Mouse Scroll Wheel (adjusts model scale or Z-depth).

* Electron Integration Flow:
  1. Add native `mousedown`, `mousemove`, `mouseup`, and `wheel` listeners directly in `renderer.js`.
  2. When mouse dragging happens:
     - Check `event.altKey` ➔ Translate coordinates delta to `characterGroup.rotation.y` and `characterGroup.rotation.x`.
     - Check `event.shiftKey` ➔ Translate coordinates delta to `characterGroup.position.x` and `characterGroup.position.y`.
     - Check `wheel` event ➔ Translate deltaY to `characterGroup.position.z` (or camera FOV/zoom).
  3. Ensure no modifier keys are held before allowing normal window-dragging behavior.

* Pros:
  - Keeps the camera statically centered, preventing model-clipping edge issues.
  - High degree of code control; does not require third-party OrbitControls dependency.
* Cons:
  - Custom camera-aligned math is required: panning up/down and left/right must take the 
    current rotation of the model into account, or it won't feel aligned with screen coordinates.
  - Lacks built-in inertia / physics damping out of the box (must be written manually).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ALTERNATIVE C: TRANSLUCENT OVERLAY GIZMO & HUD SELECTORS (UI-CENTRIC SYSTEM)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This solution overlays a Blender-like interactive visual HUD in the corner of the
window. Users do not need to memorize modifier keys; they interact with buttons.

* Mouse Interaction Mapping (Blender Sync):
  - Orbit (Rotate View): Left-click and drag inside a visual 3D spherical Coordinate Axis Gizmo.
  - Pan (Up/Down/Forward/Backward): Left-click and drag on a "Hand" Icon button.
  - Zoom (Move Forward/Backward): Left-click and drag on a "Magnifying Glass" Icon button.

* Electron Integration Flow:
  1. Create a transparent HTML HUD layer containing SVGs representing the Orbit sphere, 
     Hand, and Magnifier.
  2. Map click-drag listeners to these SVGs inside `renderer.js`.
  3. As the user drags the mouse inside the SVG bounds:
     - Sphere ➔ Rotates the 3D model.
     - Hand ➔ Translates the 3D model camera/position.
     - Magnifier ➔ Zooms camera in and out.
  4. Standard raycasting allows mouse clicks to pass through the main canvas area, ensuring the
     hud buttons only capture mouse input when the cursor is directly over them.

* Pros:
  - Visually engaging and intuitive; works seamlessly with touchpads and standard mice.
  - No keyboard combinations required, making it highly accessible.
* Cons:
  - Adds constant visual clutter to the screen overlay.
  - Higher HTML/CSS/JS layer complexity.


================================================================================
RECOMMENDED SELECTION AND ROADMAP
================================================================================
To implement a premium 3D desktop pet companion, we recommend:

1. Phase 1 (Model-Centric System):
   - Implement Alternative B for basic rotation, translation, and zooming, keeping the 
     project dependencies thin and camera alignment stable.

2. Phase 2 (Key Modifier Gestures & Skeleton/Armature Clarification):
   - Add keyboard modifier triggers (Alt/Shift) to make the navigation gestures feel exactly 
     like Blender's default hotkeys.
   - CLARIFICATION: These navigation gestures (orbit, pan, zoom) DO NOT require the 3D model 
     to have a built-in skeleton (armature). Because the system manipulates the Three.js 
     camera or the root model group container directly, it works out-of-the-box on any static 
     or un-rigged 3D model, as well as rigged ones.

3. Phase 3 (Productivity Mirroring & Posing Utility Clarification):
   - Implement the productivity mirroring concepts to add immediate functional utility.
   - CLARIFICATION: The viewport navigation system is designed so that the user can manually 
     adjust and lock in a new "static view perspective/static style" (e.g. customized camera 
     angle, position offset, and scale zoom) directly on their desktop. This serves as a 
     utility to custom-pose the mascot visually, rather than requiring the creator to author 
     and export an entirely new keyframe animation from a tool like Blender.
